Why Simplicity Wins on the Web

February 27, 2026

The modern web has a bloat problem. Megabytes of JavaScript, dozens of third-party trackers, and render-blocking fonts have made the average webpage significantly heavier than it needs to be. Users feel it every time a page takes more than three seconds to load – and most of them simply leave.

Speed Is a Feature

A plain HTML page served over a CDN can load in under 200 milliseconds. No framework warm-up, no client-side hydration, no layout shift while web fonts swap in. Visitors get instant content, and search engines reward the performance score with better rankings.

Less to Break

Every dependency you add is a potential security vulnerability, a future breaking change, and extra cognitive overhead for the next developer. Static sites with minimal dependencies age gracefully and require almost no maintenance.

The Takeaway

Before reaching for a framework, ask yourself: can I solve this with HTML and a little CSS? More often than not, the answer is yes – and your users will thank you for it.
